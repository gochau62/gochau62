Summarization of Streetwear and Designer Clothing

History of Streetwear and Designer Clothing

Streetwear in the last couple years has become a growing trend and has become a mainstream icon however, that was not always the case. Origins of Streetwear came from skaters in the late 1970s and weren't popularized until recent years. This is the same case for designer clothes as many of the brands date back over 200 years before World War II. In the 1980s, surfing and skateboarding were the two most popular cultures. People also began producing custom skateboards, surfboards, as well as t-shirts to express personality as well show different types of style of surfing and skating. Magazines began sharing the widespread of skating as well as surfing which led to the ultimate rise of streetwear culture. Designer clothing brands such as Gucci and Louis Vuitton both started out as small lugging and leather companies. They primarily focused on suitcases, and bags. In 2013 they partnered with Dior and started producing accessories which became a wildfire as many consumers hurried to purchase jackets, and other apparel. Their eventual growth from a bag company to a widespread clothing brand was transformed because of major partnership between Louis Vuitton and Dior.

Celebrities, musicians, athletes, all play a crucial role in marketing for clothing, their influence while wearing different styles, brands, and accessories influences the urban culture and created a community who strives to create unique styles and different blends of flavor with fashion.
